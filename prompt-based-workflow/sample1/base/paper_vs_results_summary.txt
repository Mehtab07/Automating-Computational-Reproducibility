# 1. **Key Expectations from the Paper**

## a. **Sample & Descriptives**
- N = 262 (131 dyads)
- Both self- and friend-reports on Big Five and 10 fundamental motives (FSMI).
- Items rated on 1–7 Likert scales.
- Reliability (α) for BFI: ≈.80; for FSMI: .68–.91.
- Means and SDs for traits/motives in Table 2 (e.g., Self-protection Self: M ≈ 4.7, SD ≈ 1.1).

## b. **Analytic Approach**
- Uses the Social Accuracy Model (SAM): 
  - Multilevel regression (mixed models, lme4 in R)
  - Predictors: distinctive accuracy (target self), normativity (item mean), social desirability (item desirability), assumed similarity (perceiver self), all item-centered.
  - Random slopes for each effect by perceiver (PID).

## c. **Primary Findings**
- **Distinctive accuracy** exists for both traits (b ≈ .29) and motives (b ≈ .23), but is higher for traits (difference b ≈ .07).
- **Normativity** (b ≈ .80 traits, .84 motives) is higher for motives.
- **Social desirability** (b ≈ .25 traits, .07 motives) is lower for motives.
- **Assumed similarity** (b ≈ .15 traits, .25 motives) is higher for motives.
- **Visibility**: Motives are less visible than traits. Visibility partly explains lower accuracy for motives.
- **Relationship with friendship quality**: Accuracy for motives (but not traits) predicts friendship quality; normativity and assumed similarity also positive; social desirability not predictive.
